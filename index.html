Into The Woods

Let's go on an adventure, shall we?

One day, I decided to follow my dog, Roxy, into the woods.

She loves going into the woods and usually I yell at her not to. But that day, I decided to follow after her instead of holler after her.

It was a brisk, cold afternoon near the end of November. I was bundled up in my fleece-lined leggings, sweatshirt, coat, and another large flannel coat over that one. Can't forget gloves and a warm headband, too.

I had the fleeting thought to text my husband and let him know. Just in case I fell or went missing.

Nov 23 2020, approx. 12:00pm: I went into the woods with Roxy fyi.

As we meandered, leaves rustling around our feet and paws, we began down a sloping hill into the ravine. I was careful where I stepped and held onto tiny trees for support. Roxy rushed forward excitedly.

I very soon came across a fallen tree that made for a perfect place to sit.

I started singing "Sitting on a log in the woods" to the tune of "Sitting on the dock of the bay".

I looked around as Roxy explored her surroundings. The sky was a gorgeous blue and practically cloudless. The sun shone high and bright, rays filtered through the naked branches. I sent another text to my husband.

Nov 23 2020, approx. 12:05pm: It's nice out here lol. Roxy is staying close and just sniffing and roaming.

Roxy came over after sniffing every rock and leaf and stick to her satisfaction and decided to sit as well.

She's majestic.

After around 20 minutes and some more meandering, we decided to head back to the house.

Following my dog into the woods was probaby the best decision I've made for my mental health all year.
